MATH (for 800) - ADVANCED CONCEPTS
================================================================================

1. ADVANCED ALGEBRA - The Nuances That Matter
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

RATIONAL EXPRESSIONS - Beyond Basic Factoring
  ⚠️ TRICKY: When does simplification create restrictions?
  
  Example: (x² - 9)/(x + 3) = x - 3, BUT x ≠ -3
  
  Key Insight: The simplified form looks different but has domain restrictions
  from the original. Questions test whether you remember these restrictions.
  
  Common Trap: Substituting x = -3 into simplified form and getting an answer
  when the original expression is undefined.

SYSTEMS WITH INFINITELY MANY SOLUTIONS
  ⚠️ TRICKY: When do two equations represent the same line?
  
  For infinitely many solutions: Equations must be proportional
  Example: 2x + 4y = 6 and 3x + 6y = 9 are the same line
  
  Key Insight: All coefficients must have the same ratio, including constant term
  
  Common Trap: Thinking equations are different when they're just scaled versions

FUNCTION COMPOSITION NOTATION
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Order matters! f(g(x)) ≠ g(f(x))
  
  Example: If f(x) = x² and g(x) = x + 1
  - f(g(x)) = (x + 1)² = x² + 2x + 1
  - g(f(x)) = x² + 1  [Different!]
  
  Key Insight: Work from inside out. For f(g(x)), do g first, then f
  
  Common Trap: Switching the order or trying to "distribute" the composition


2. QUADRATICS - Subtle Properties
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

VERTEX FORM NUANCES
  ⚠️ TRICKY: y = a(x - h)² + k
  
  The signs matter:
  - (x - 3)² means vertex at x = 3 [minus in formula, positive h]
  - (x + 3)² means vertex at x = -3 [plus in formula, negative h]
  
  Key Insight: The formula has (x - h), so you need opposite sign
  
  Common Trap: Thinking (x + 3)² has vertex at +3

PARABOLA PROPERTIES
  ⚠️ TRICKY: How a, h, k each affect the graph
  
  - a > 0: opens up; a < 0: opens down
  - |a| > 1: narrower; |a| < 1: wider
  - h: horizontal shift (opposite of sign in formula)
  - k: vertical shift (same as sign)
  
  Key Insight: Questions often ask about transformations in tricky ways
  
  Common Trap: Confusing horizontal and vertical shifts

DISCRIMINANT FOR SOLUTIONS
  ⚠️ TRICKY: b² - 4ac tells you about solutions WITHOUT solving
  
  - b² - 4ac > 0: Two real solutions
  - b² - 4ac = 0: One repeated solution (vertex on x-axis)
  - b² - 4ac < 0: No real solutions
  
  Key Insight: This is faster than completing the square or quadratic formula
  
  Common Trap: Forgetting to include the -4ac part


3. FUNCTIONS - Domain, Range, and Transformations
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

DOMAIN RESTRICTIONS
  ⚠️ TRICKY: What values make expressions undefined?
  
  Cannot have:
  - Division by zero: x/(x-3) undefined at x = 3
  - Square root of negative: √(x-5) requires x ≥ 5
  - Even roots of negatives: ⁴√(x+2) requires x ≥ -2
  
  Key Insight: Find restrictions BEFORE simplifying
  
  Common Trap: Simplifying first and losing track of original restrictions

FUNCTION TRANSFORMATIONS
  ⚠️ TRICKY: f(x + 2) vs f(x) + 2 vs 2f(x)
  
  Starting with f(x):
  - f(x) + k: Shift UP k units [outside]
  - f(x + h): Shift LEFT h units [inside, opposite sign]
  - a·f(x): Vertical stretch by a [outside]
  - f(a·x): Horizontal compression by a [inside]
  
  Key Insight: Inside changes affect x (horizontal), outside affects y (vertical)
  
  Common Trap: Horizontal shifts go OPPOSITE direction of sign

INVERSE FUNCTIONS
  ⚠️ TRICKY: If f(3) = 7, then f⁻¹(7) = 3
  
  Key Insight: Inverse "undoes" the function, swapping input and output
  
  Common Trap: Thinking f⁻¹(x) = 1/f(x) [That's reciprocal, not inverse!]


4. EXPONENTIALS AND GROWTH - The Subtle Distinctions
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

GROWTH RATE VS. GROWTH FACTOR
  ⚠️ TRICKY: y = 100(1.05)ᵗ
  
  - Growth FACTOR: 1.05 (multiply by this each time)
  - Growth RATE: 0.05 or 5% (the increase)
  - Rate = Factor - 1
  
  Key Insight: Questions ask for "growth rate" but you might calculate factor
  
  Common Trap: Saying growth rate is 1.05 instead of 0.05

EXPONENTIAL DECAY
  ⚠️ TRICKY: y = 100(0.85)ᵗ represents decay, not 15% growth
  
  - If base < 1: Decay
  - Decay rate = 1 - base = 1 - 0.85 = 0.15 = 15%
  
  Key Insight: Base of 0.85 means 85% remains (15% lost)
  
  Common Trap: Thinking 0.85 means 85% growth

COMPOUND INTEREST NUANCES
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Annual vs. monthly compounding matters
  
  Formula: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)
  - n = number of times compounded per year
  - If monthly: n = 12
  - If quarterly: n = 4
  
  Key Insight: More frequent compounding = more interest
  
  Common Trap: Using wrong n value or forgetting to divide r by n


5. STATISTICS - Beyond the Basics
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

MEAN VS. MEDIAN IN SKEWED DATA
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Which measure is affected by outliers?
  
  - Mean: Affected by outliers (pulled toward extreme values)
  - Median: Resistant to outliers (only cares about middle position)
  
  Key Insight: If data is skewed right, mean > median
  
  Common Trap: Thinking mean is always "better" measure

STANDARD DEVIATION INTERPRETATION
  ⚠️ TRICKY: What does standard deviation actually tell you?
  
  - Measures spread/variability
  - Larger SD = more spread out
  - Two data sets can have same mean but different SDs
  
  Key Insight: SD = 0 means all values are identical
  
  Common Trap: Confusing standard deviation with mean

CORRELATION VS. CAUSATION
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Just because two things correlate doesn't mean one causes the other
  
  Example: Ice cream sales and drowning deaths correlate
  Reason: Both increase in summer (confounding variable)
  
  Key Insight: SAT loves to ask "what can be concluded" - usually very little!
  
  Common Trap: Assuming correlation implies causation

MARGIN OF ERROR
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Larger sample → smaller margin of error
  
  Key relationship: Margin of error ∝ 1/√n
  - To halve margin of error: Need 4× sample size
  - To cut by 1/3: Need 9× sample size
  
  Key Insight: Quadruple sample size to halve margin of error
  
  Common Trap: Thinking linear relationship (2× sample → 1/2 error)


6. GEOMETRY & TRIGONOMETRY - The Nuanced Properties
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CIRCLE EQUATIONS
  ⚠️ TRICKY: (x - h)² + (y - k)² = r²
  
  Center is at (h, k) where h and k have OPPOSITE signs from equation
  Example: (x + 3)² + (y - 2)² = 25
  - Center: (-3, 2) [opposite signs!]
  - Radius: 5 [square root of 25]
  
  Key Insight: Same sign reversal as vertex form
  
  Common Trap: Thinking center is at (3, -2)

RADIAN VS. DEGREE MEASURE
  ⚠️ TRICKY: π radians = 180°
  
  Common conversions:
  - π/2 = 90°
  - π/3 = 60°
  - π/4 = 45°
  - π/6 = 30°
  
  Key Insight: Radians are arc length for unit circle
  
  Common Trap: Mixing up which mode calculator is in

SPECIAL RIGHT TRIANGLES
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Must recognize ratios instantly
  
  45-45-90: sides in ratio 1 : 1 : √2
  30-60-90: sides in ratio 1 : √3 : 2
  
  Key Insight: These appear constantly. Memorize them!
  
  Common Trap: Applying ratios to wrong triangle type

COMPLEMENTARY ANGLES IN TRIG
  ⚠️ TRICKY: sin(x) = cos(90° - x)
  
  - sin(30°) = cos(60°)
  - tan(x) · tan(90° - x) = 1
  
  Key Insight: Complementary angle relationships are tested frequently
  
  Common Trap: Not recognizing these relationships quickly


7. COMPLEX WORD PROBLEMS - Multi-Step Reasoning
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

RATE PROBLEMS WITH MULTIPLE WORKERS
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Rates add, times don't
  
  If A does job in 3 hours (rate = 1/3 job/hour)
  And B does job in 6 hours (rate = 1/6 job/hour)
  Together: 1/3 + 1/6 = 1/2 job/hour → 2 hours total
  
  Key Insight: Add RATES, not times
  
  Common Trap: Averaging the times (saying 4.5 hours)

MIXTURE PROBLEMS
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Track amount of substance, not just volume
  
  Example: Mix 20% solution with 50% solution
  Must track: (concentration × volume) for each
  
  Key Insight: Set up equation based on amount of pure substance
  
  Common Trap: Averaging percentages without considering volumes

PERCENT INCREASE/DECREASE CHAINS
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Increases and decreases don't cancel
  
  Example: Increase 20%, then decrease 20%
  - Start: 100
  - After +20%: 120
  - After -20%: 96 [NOT back to 100!]
  
  Key Insight: Percentages apply to current value, not original
  
  Common Trap: Thinking +20% then -20% returns to start

ENGLISH (for 800) - ADVANCED CONCEPTS
================================================================================

1. RHETORICAL SYNTHESIS - Comparing Complex Arguments
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

IDENTIFYING SUBTLE AGREEMENTS
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Two authors can disagree on conclusion but agree on facts
  
  Example:
  - Author 1: "Technology increases productivity but reduces jobs"
  - Author 2: "Technology creates new jobs in different sectors"
  
  They AGREE: Technology affects employment
  They DISAGREE: Whether effect is net positive
  
  Key Insight: Look for COMMON GROUND, not identical positions
  
  Common Trap: Saying they disagree about everything

DISTINGUISHING TYPES OF DISAGREEMENT
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Are they disagreeing about facts or interpretation?
  
  - Factual disagreement: Different data or observations
  - Interpretive disagreement: Same facts, different conclusions
  - Methodological disagreement: Different approaches to study
  
  Key Insight: SAT often asks what TYPE of disagreement exists
  
  Common Trap: Oversimplifying to "they disagree"

RESPONSE QUESTIONS
  ⚠️ TRICKY: "How would Author 2 respond to Author 1's claim?"
  
  Must base answer on:
  - What Author 2 actually says
  - Logical extension of Author 2's position
  
  Key Insight: Answer must be SUPPORTED by Author 2's text
  
  Common Trap: Choosing what YOU think they'd say, not what text supports


2. INFERENCES - Drawing Valid Conclusions
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

THE MOST DEFENSIBLE INFERENCE
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Choose the inference that requires FEWEST assumptions
  
  Text: "Sales increased 40% after the advertising campaign"
  
  Too strong: "The advertising caused the increase" [assumes causation]
  Too weak: "Something happened" [too vague]
  CORRECT: "Sales and advertising timing are associated" [just what's stated]
  
  Key Insight: SAT wants minimal, defensible inferences
  
  Common Trap: Making logical leaps beyond what text supports

"MOST STRONGLY SUGGESTS"
  ⚠️ TRICKY: All answers might be possible, but one has BEST support
  
  Example: "The experiment failed three times before succeeding"
  
  Weak: Researcher is incompetent [not supported]
  Medium: Research is difficult [reasonable but not explicit]
  STRONG: Multiple attempts were needed [directly stated]
  
  Key Insight: Choose answer closest to actual text
  
  Common Trap: Choosing interesting answer instead of best-supported

DISTINGUISHING IMPLIED VS. STATED
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Is this explicitly said or just implied?
  
  Text: "Though the budget was limited, the team completed the project"
  
  Stated: Budget was limited, project completed
  Implied: Team was resourceful/effective
  NOT SUPPORTED: Budget should have been larger
  
  Key Insight: Inference questions want IMPLICATIONS, not restatements
  
  Common Trap: Choosing answer that just restates the text


3. TEXT STRUCTURE AND FUNCTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

FUNCTION OF A SENTENCE
  ⚠️ TRICKY: What ROLE does this sentence play in the argument?
  
  Common functions:
  - Present main claim
  - Offer evidence for claim
  - Acknowledge counterargument
  - Refute opposing view
  - Qualify or limit a claim
  - Provide example
  
  Key Insight: Think about argument structure, not just content
  
  Common Trap: Summarizing what sentence SAYS instead of what it DOES

"MAINLY SERVE TO..."
  ⚠️ TRICKY: A paragraph can do multiple things, but one is PRIMARY
  
  Example paragraph discusses benefits of policy, then mentions costs
  
  Primarily: Present benefits [most of paragraph]
  Secondarily: Acknowledge limitations [one sentence]
  
  Key Insight: Focus on what gets most attention/space
  
  Common Trap: Focusing on interesting details instead of main purpose

OVERALL STRUCTURE
  ⚠️ TRICKY: How is the whole passage organized?
  
  Common structures:
  - Problem/solution
  - Chronological narrative
  - Compare/contrast
  - Claim + supporting evidence
  - Question → investigation → answer
  
  Key Insight: Identify the "skeleton" of the argument
  
  Common Trap: Getting lost in details instead of seeing big picture


4. COMMAND OF EVIDENCE - Which Quote Best Supports
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

BEST VS. GOOD EVIDENCE
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Multiple quotes might support claim, but one is STRONGEST
  
  Claim: "The policy was controversial"
  
  Weak: "Some concerns were raised" [vague]
  Medium: "Opponents strongly criticized it" [better but one-sided]
  BEST: "It sparked heated debate with strong views on both sides"
  
  Key Insight: Best evidence is most DIRECT and COMPLETE
  
  Common Trap: Choosing first quote that seems relevant

MATCHING SPECIFICITY
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Evidence must match the SCOPE of the claim
  
  Broad claim: "Deforestation harms biodiversity"
  Need: General statement about species loss
  Not: Specific example of one endangered species
  
  Specific claim: "Tigers are threatened by habitat loss"
  Need: Data about tigers specifically
  Not: General statement about all species
  
  Key Insight: Match generality of evidence to generality of claim
  
  Common Trap: Using interesting but wrong-scope evidence


5. WORDS IN CONTEXT - Subtle Distinctions
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SIMILAR BUT NOT IDENTICAL WORDS
  ⚠️ TRICKY: "Assert" vs. "suggest" vs. "imply" vs. "state"
  
  - Assert: Claim confidently
  - Suggest: Indicate without stating directly
  - Imply: Hint at indirectly
  - State: Say explicitly
  
  Key Insight: Pay attention to strength and directness
  
  Common Trap: Treating all similar words as interchangeable

CONNOTATION MATTERS
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Neutral vs. positive vs. negative shading
  
  Example: "cheap" vs. "inexpensive" vs. "economical"
  All mean low cost, but:
  - Cheap: Negative (poor quality)
  - Inexpensive: Neutral
  - Economical: Positive (good value)
  
  Key Insight: Context determines which shade of meaning fits
  
  Common Trap: Ignoring emotional/evaluative overtones

TECHNICAL VS. COMMON MEANING
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Some words have special meanings in academic contexts
  
  "Reproduce" in sociology: Perpetuate social structures
  "Significant" in statistics: Unlikely due to chance
  "Justify" in typography: Align to margins
  
  Key Insight: Consider the academic discipline
  
  Common Trap: Always using everyday definition


6. TRANSITIONS - The Right Word for the Relationship
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

LOGICAL RELATIONSHIPS
  ⚠️ TRICKY: What's the actual relationship between ideas?
  
  Cause/effect: therefore, thus, consequently, as a result
  Contrast: however, nevertheless, yet, although, whereas
  Addition: moreover, furthermore, additionally, also
  Example: for instance, for example, specifically
  Emphasis: indeed, in fact, certainly
  
  Key Insight: First determine relationship, THEN pick transition
  
  Common Trap: Choosing transition that sounds good but wrong relationship

SUBTLE CONTRASTS
  ⚠️ TRICKY: "However" vs. "Nevertheless" vs. "On the other hand"
  
  - However: General contrast
  - Nevertheless: Contrast despite expectation
  - On the other hand: Presenting alternative view
  
  Key Insight: Level of contrast varies
  
  Common Trap: Using strongest contrast for mild disagreement

NO TRANSITION NEEDED
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Sometimes the right answer is DELETE
  
  If ideas flow naturally, transition can be redundant or awkward
  
  Key Insight: "No transition" is valid answer choice
  
  Common Trap: Thinking you must always add a transition


7. GRAMMAR - Subtle Errors Hard to Catch
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SUBJECT-VERB AGREEMENT WITH INTERRUPTION
  ⚠️ TRICKY: The team, along with its coach, WAS celebrating
  
  Subject is "team" (singular), not "team and coach"
  "Along with its coach" is interrupting phrase
  
  Key Insight: Mentally remove words between subject and verb
  
  Common Trap: Matching verb to nearest noun instead of actual subject

PRONOUN-ANTECEDENT AGREEMENT
  ⚠️ TRICKY: "Each student must bring THEIR book" is WRONG
  
  "Each" is singular → requires "his or her" (or rewrite)
  Modern SAT may accept singular "they" but test carefully
  
  Key Insight: Words like "each," "every," "anyone" are SINGULAR
  
  Common Trap: Using plural pronoun with singular indefinite pronoun

MISPLACED MODIFIERS
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Modifier must be next to what it modifies
  
  WRONG: "Running down the street, the car nearly hit me"
  [The car wasn't running!]
  
  RIGHT: "Running down the street, I was nearly hit by the car"
  [I was running]
  
  Key Insight: Opening modifier must refer to sentence subject
  
  Common Trap: Not checking what word immediately follows modifier

PARALLEL STRUCTURE IN LISTS
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Items in list must have same grammatical form
  
  WRONG: "I like reading, to write, and hiking"
  RIGHT: "I like reading, writing, and hiking" [all gerunds]
  OR: "I like to read, to write, and to hike" [all infinitives]
  
  Key Insight: List items must match grammatically
  
  Common Trap: Mixing gerunds, infinitives, and other forms

COMMA SPLICES AND RUN-ONS
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Two independent clauses need proper connection
  
  WRONG: "The test was hard, I studied for hours"
  [Comma splice - comma alone can't join independent clauses]
  
  RIGHT options:
  - Period: "The test was hard. I studied for hours."
  - Semicolon: "The test was hard; I studied for hours."
  - Conjunction: "The test was hard, so I studied for hours."
  
  Key Insight: Comma alone can't join complete sentences
  
  Common Trap: Thinking comma is enough


8. PUNCTUATION - Advanced Usage
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SEMICOLON RULES
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Only between independent clauses OR in complex lists
  
  Correct uses:
  - "I studied; she slept" [Two complete sentences]
  - "Attendees included Dr. Smith, Boston; Dr. Lee, Chicago; and Dr. Patel, Mumbai"
    [List items contain commas]
  
  WRONG: "Because I studied; I passed" [Not two independent clauses]
  
  Key Insight: Both sides of semicolon must be complete sentences
  
  Common Trap: Using semicolon before dependent clause

COLON USAGE
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Colon introduces explanation, list, or elaboration
  
  MUST have complete sentence before colon:
  
  RIGHT: "I need three things: milk, eggs, bread"
  [Complete sentence before colon]
  
  WRONG: "I need: milk, eggs, and bread"
  [Incomplete before colon]
  
  Key Insight: What comes before colon must stand alone
  
  Common Trap: Using colon after linking verb or preposition

EM-DASH FOR EMPHASIS
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Em-dash sets off information for emphasis
  
  "The solution—a simple change in procedure—saved millions"
  
  Key Insight: Like parentheses but MORE emphatic
  
  Common Trap: Confusing em-dash with hyphen or en-dash

APOSTROPHES IN POSSESSIVES
  ⚠️ TRICKY: Its vs. it's; your vs. you're
  
  It's = it is (contraction)
  Its = belonging to it (possessive)
  
  You're = you are (contraction)
  Your = belonging to you (possessive)
  
  Key Insight: Possessive pronouns NEVER have apostrophes
  
  Common Trap: Adding apostrophe to possessive pronoun

STUDY STRATEGIES FOR MASTERY
================================================================================

1. ACTIVE PRACTICE
  - Don't just read concepts - DO problems testing each concept
  - Keep error log of mistakes by concept
  - Redo problems you got wrong after 3-4 days

2. TIMED PRESSURE
  - Practice under real time constraints
  - Learn to identify question types quickly
  - Develop shortcuts for common patterns

3. ANALYZE WRONG ANSWERS
  - Why was each wrong answer tempting?
  - What trap did test makers set?
  - What would make you pick wrong answer again?

4. FOCUS ON PATTERNS
  - SAT reuses question structures
  - Learn to recognize "question types"
  - Same concepts appear repeatedly

5. PRECISION OVER SPEED
  - Better to get 98% right slowly than 95% right quickly
  - For 1600, accuracy matters more than finishing every question
  - Take time to check tricky problems
